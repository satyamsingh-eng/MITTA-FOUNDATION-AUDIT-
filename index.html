VERDICT Executive Summary — 30 Seconds

The website is broken at its two most critical points.

Mitta Foundation has been doing genuine, documented humanitarian work since 2006. The website does not represent that work. It contains finance template content, a non-functional donation system, blank forms, and broken navigation. Every day it remains in this state, real donor money is lost.

The gap between Mitta's real-world impact and its digital representation is the single biggest constraint on its growth right now. A focused redesign — rooted in donor psychology, transparency, and storytelling — can unlock online donations as a recurring revenue stream, establish national and international credibility, and position Mitta alongside globally respected NGOs in structure, tone, and professionalism.
01 — The Vision

A robust US website for Mitta Foundation.

The goal is to establish a credible, US-based digital home for the Mitta Foundation — a website that represents the ongoing India work to an audience of US donors, diaspora supporters, and corporate philanthropy partners.

Three things this website must do

  1. Target US donors specifically. The language, tone, tax framing, and giving flow must be built for a US audience — not adapted from the India site. US donors have different trust expectations, different tax requirements, and different baseline standards for what a credible NGO website looks like.
  2. Connect transparently to the India work. The US site is not a separate organisation. It is the front door for US supporters to the ongoing programs, beneficiaries, and field operations in India. Every claim on the US site must be traceable to real work on the ground.
  3. Address US tax and donation requirements. US donors benchmark against 501(c)(3) NGOs. The site must handle tax-deductibility framing, multiple giving mechanisms (single donation, monthly sponsorship, corporate giving), and the compliance signals that make an international nonprofit look legitimate to an American giver.

The US website is not a translation of the India website. It is a purpose-built donor conversion engine for a different audience, built to a different standard.

03 — Failure Breakdown

Four structural failure areas. Each independently damaging.

3.1 Trust Failures

  • No team page, no board of directors, no named leadership — anonymous organisations do not receive large donations
  • No annual report, no fund usage breakdown, no 80G/12A certificate information displayed
  • No beneficiary stories with verifiable details — gallery photos exist but carry zero captions, dates, or context
  • Copyright footer reads 2024 — signals the site has not been maintained in over a year
  • Founder name misspelled in the About page ("Swatatntrakala") — undermines the credibility of the site's most important trust asset

3.2 UX & Navigation Failures

  • Social Services nav link — 404 Not Found. This is the core programs page.
  • Mitta Skill Academy nav link — 404 Not Found. A named programme with no page.
  • "Sponsor A Child" CTA button routes to the Contact Us page, not a giving flow
  • No mission statement above the fold — hero section has images but does not answer: Who are you? What do you do? Who do you serve?
  • No impact statistics anywhere on the homepage — a foundation with 18+ years of work shows nothing quantified

3.3 Donation System Failures

  • No working donation gateway — the single most critical failure on the entire site
  • Right column of the /donate-now/ page is a blank white box — no form, no widget, no bank details
  • No suggested donation amounts, no impact-per-amount framing ("₹1,500 = one child's education for a month")
  • No recurring donation option — the highest-value donor segment is entirely ignored
  • No post-donation confirmation, receipt, or follow-up flow exists

3.4 Content Failures

  • Grammar error: "childs education" appears verbatim on every page via a shared CTA component
  • "Together for a Brighter Tomorrow" — a subheading with no specificity. Does not communicate mission, geography, or beneficiary group
  • No beneficiary stories on any page — the most powerful conversion content an NGO can publish is absent throughout
  • Project list is too generic: "Construction of new classrooms" — no school name, no location, no date, no investment figure
04 — Benchmark Insights

What leading NGOs do differently.

Patterns observed across Charity: Water, GiveDirectly, Akshaya Patra, Smile Foundation, CRY India, and Doctors Without Borders.

What they all have that Mitta currently lacks

01
3-second mission clarity
Who / what / where is visible on the homepage hero in under 3 seconds. No visitor has to scroll to understand the organisation's purpose. Mitta's hero answers none of these questions.
02
Transparency as a top nav item
Annual reports, fund allocation, and audited financials are one click from any page. Transparency is treated as a conversion tool, not a legal obligation.
03
Beneficiary-first storytelling
"Meet Aarav, age 7" not "We are committed to education." One named story with a photo converts more visitors than ten paragraphs of mission copy.
04
Donation amounts tied to outcomes
Every suggested amount has a specific impact statement. ₹500 = school books for a year. ₹2,000 = a month of vocational training. This specificity drives conversion at 2–3x the rate of generic asks.

Voice and framing

  • Voice: Warm, grounded, confident. Not corporate. Not saviour-toned. The founders grew up in poverty — this voice is already authentic to them
  • Tense: Present and specific. "Today, in Gudimalkapur, Priya walked into her first classroom" beats "We are committed to education"
  • Structure: Beneficiary-first, not organisation-first. The story of one child converts more than ten paragraphs of mission copy
  • Evidence: Every emotional claim must be paired with a number or a name. Vague sincerity without proof is indistinguishable from fiction

Founder story — deploy at three layers

  1. Homepage — 4-line note with founder photo, linking to the full story
  2. About page — full narrative with photos of the founders, timeline of the foundation's growth
  3. Donation confirmation page — personal thank-you from the founder, perceived as handwritten

Impact storytelling system

Build a repeatable content engine that compounds over time:

  • One story per month, structured identically: Who → Before → Intervention → After → What's next
  • Each story becomes: homepage feature → blog post → Instagram carousel → donor email update
  • Archive into a permanent Story Library — this compounds over years and becomes a trust moat that no competitor can replicate
  • The School Enlightenment page already has the structure right — specific schools, specific rupee amounts, specific years. This model should be applied everywhere

Mitta has the substance — real work, real impact, a real founding story. The redesign is primarily a signalling upgrade, not a content invention. The work exists. The website needs to finally be worthy of it.

07 — Design System

The signalling layer, engineered for donor trust.

The design system must do one job: make a first-time visitor feel that Mitta is real, trustworthy, and worth giving to within 30 seconds of landing.

7.1 Colour Palette

Deep Trust Blue#0A3A66 · Primary
Soft Ivory#F7F4EE · Background
Warm Amber#C8823A · Accent/CTA
Charcoal#1C1C1C · Body text
Muted Green#2F7A5C · Success
Role Colour Psychological function
Primary Deep Trust Blue #0A3A66 Universal colour of institutional trust (UN, hospitals, universities). Signals maturity and permanence, not corporate coldness.
Background Soft Ivory #F7F4EE Warm off-white. Feels humanitarian and editorial, not sterile. Reduces cognitive strain during reading.
Accent / CTA Warm Amber #C8823A Triggers warmth, generosity, human light. Used on donate buttons and CTAs — drives action. Amber evokes harvest and community.
Body Text Charcoal #1C1C1C Higher readability than pure black. Softer, more approachable at scale.
Success Muted Green #2F7A5C For impact counters, confirmation states, and "money well spent" signals.

7.2 Typography

Role Typeface Why it works
Display / Headlines Fraunces (optical serif) Serif headlines signal editorial credibility, heritage, and humanity — the opposite of a corporate template. Fraunces has warmth without nostalgia.
Body / UI Inter (sans-serif) Neutral, highly legible, modern. Trusted by premium publications and global NGOs. Open-source and widely supported.
Numbers / Impact Inter Bold Tabular Clean tabular figures for impact counters, amounts, and financial data.

7.3 Layout principles

  • Generous whitespace — breathing room signals confidence and care. Every crowded section reads as anxiety, not impact
  • One idea per section — no dense multi-column homepage blocks competing for attention
  • Photography-led — authentic field imagery is the hero, not decoration. Mitta's existing gallery photos are strong enough
  • Sticky donate bar on mobile (bottom) and desktop (top-right) — the donation intent window is short, the mechanism must always be visible
  • Human eye contact in hero imagery — biologically proven to increase trust and donation intent by 20–30%
08 — Final Conclusion

What changes. And why it matters.

After these fixes

  • A first-time visitor will understand Mitta's mission, see proof of its impact, and find a working, reassuring donation flow — all within 30 seconds
  • The website will stop leaking donor intent and start converting it
  • Mitta will move from "a small NGO with a broken site" to "a credible institution with a clear story and a transparent ask"
  • The organisation will gain a compounding content asset that grows its trust and donor base year over year

Why this matters for global positioning

NGOs today compete for attention in the same feed as Charity: Water, GiveDirectly, and UNICEF. Donors — especially younger, digital-native, diaspora, and corporate donors — benchmark unconsciously. A website that doesn't meet global standards is quietly disqualified, regardless of the quality of work on the ground.

Mitta's real-world work already deserves that league. The redesign is simply the act of making the digital presence match the truth of the work.

The goal is not a prettier website. The goal is a website that is finally worthy of the work Mitta is already doing.
09 — Strategic Recommendations

Sequenced by urgency and impact.

A
Immediate · This Week
Stop the revenue bleeding
  • Integrate a working donation gateway — Razorpay for India, PayPal or Stripe for international. Even a minimal embed on a blank page is infinitely better than nothing
  • As a fallback: publish bank account details, UPI ID, and a QR code on the donate page immediately
  • Fix or remove the "Enquiry Form" heading on Contact Us — either add real fields or remove the heading so the page isn't broken
  • Fix the sitewide CTA: "childs education" → "children's education"
  • Fix founder name typo: "Swatatntrakala" → "Swatantrakala"
  • Either build or remove "Social Services" and "Mitta Skill Academy" from the nav — a dead link in the primary nav is a trust killer
B
Short-term · 30–60 Days
Build the trust layer
  • Add FCRA 368140126, 80G certificate number, and 12A registration prominently on the Donate page and in the footer
  • Add impact-per-amount framing to the donate page: what does ₹500, ₹2,000, ₹10,000 actually achieve for a real child?
  • Publish the founding story prominently on the homepage — it is the site's strongest trust asset and is currently buried on page 2
  • Add captions to every gallery photo: school name, year, program name, location — uncaptioned photos read as stock imagery
  • Build a real volunteer/partner application form on the Engage page
  • Add 3–5 real impact statistics to the homepage: children reached, schools supported, years active, rupees invested
  • Fix the YouTube social link URL
C
Medium-term · 1–3 Months
Build the credibility architecture
  • Publish at least one annual report or impact summary — even a well-formatted PDF is a significant credibility signal for institutional funders
  • Build 2–3 full beneficiary stories: name, photo (with consent), before/after narrative, direct quote. This is the highest-ROI content investment available
  • Launch a monthly giving program with tiered amounts — recurring donors are the highest lifetime-value segment
  • Add a team/leadership page: founder photos, brief bios, advisory board if any. Named humans = trust
  • Create a Transparency page: fund allocation pie chart, project timeline, legal certificates download
  • Implement automated tax-receipt emails post-donation
D
Long-term · 3–12 Months
Build the growth engine
  • Full redesign: replace the current WordPress template with an NGO-appropriate design system (see Section 08 for the full design blueprint)
  • Build an Impact Dashboard — live or quarterly-updated counters showing real numbers
  • Build a Story Library — one new beneficiary story per month, compounding trust over years
  • Launch corporate partnership and CSR landing pages — the Indian CSR market is substantial and Mitta's work qualifies
  • Internationalise: English + Telugu, multi-currency donation support for diaspora
  • Pursue third-party certifications: GuideStar India, Credibility Alliance, Give India listing
